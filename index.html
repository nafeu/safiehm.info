Safieh Moghaddam

1265 Military Trail · Scarborough, ON M1C 1A4 · (416) 287-7529 · safieh.moghaddam@mail.utoronto.ca

I am an experienced lecturer in both small and large enrolment courses with a passion for generating and refining educational materials and methods. My areas of expertise include syntax, morphology, sociolinguistics, in particular, agreement systems, clitics, endangered languages, minority languages, and language and gender.

I've been teaching several distinct courses in linguistics at the University of Toronto as well as communication courses at Seneca College. I am the recipient of two teaching awards and of numerous teaching grants which I have used to redesign courses, and integrate more use of technology.

I'm a native speaker of Persian, and I am also fluent in Dari, Arabic and have a working knowledge of French, Hebrew and Kurdish. In addition, I have fieldwork experience with many languages including: Davani, Lamnso', Sorani, Assyrian, Brahui, Meymandi, etc.

I look forward to undertaking more projects regarding pedagogical methods and classroom management as well as contributing to the growth of research in linguistics.
